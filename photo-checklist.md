# Photo Checklist

Use this as a shot list. Rename files to match the suggested filenames after you take them.

## Functional / practical parts

- [ ] **Cable clip mounted under a desk** — `functional-part.jpg`
  - Show the print doing a real job, not just sitting on a table.
- [ ] **Replacement knob on a drawer, cabinet, tool, or appliance** — `replacement-bracket.jpg`
  - Take one close photo and one wider photo showing where it is used.
- [ ] **Wall hook, small bracket, spacer, or mount holding something** — `mounted-part.jpg`
  - Good for showing strength and usefulness.

## Printer/process shots

- [ ] **Print still attached to the printer bed** — `printer-bed-print.jpg`
  - Take this right after a successful print finishes.
- [ ] **Close-up of clean layers, texture, or detail** — `detail-closeup.jpg`
  - Fill the frame with the object so people can see print quality.
- [ ] **Printer running with the nozzle mid-print** — `printer-in-progress.jpg`
  - Good behind-the-scenes proof that you make these yourself.

## Small batch examples

- [ ] **Five to ten matching clips, tokens, brackets, or tags lined up** — `small-batch.jpg`
  - Arrange them neatly in rows.
- [ ] **A repeated item in different colors** — `color-batch.jpg`
  - Good for showing customization.

## Hobby / gift / decorative prints

- [ ] **Tabletop terrain, miniature stand, dice tower, or game accessory** — `tabletop-model.jpg`
  - Place it near dice, cards, or a game mat for context.
- [ ] **Nameplate, keychain, ornament, sign, or small display piece** — `gift-print.jpg`
  - Choose something that looks personal or giftable.
- [ ] **Desk toy, planter, vase, or decorative model** — `decorative-print.jpg`
  - Keep the background clean and simple.

## Organization / household prints

- [ ] **Drawer organizer, tray, bin divider, or small parts holder** — `organizer-holder.jpg`
  - Put real items inside it: screws, tools, cables, pens, etc.
- [ ] **Phone stand, controller holder, headphone hook, or cable guide** — `desk-accessory.jpg`
  - Show it in use on a desk.

## Prototype / design examples

- [ ] **Simple enclosure, test-fit shell, jig, or fixture** — `prototype-fit-check.jpg`
  - Include a ruler, calipers, screw, or component for scale.
- [ ] **Two versions of the same part side by side** — `prototype-iteration.jpg`
  - Shows iteration and problem-solving.

## Repair examples

- [ ] **Broken original part next to the printed replacement** — `repair-before-after.jpg`
  - Best if the replacement is installed in a second shot.
- [ ] **Printed adapter connecting two things that did not fit before** — `adapter-repair.jpg`
  - Good practical example for non-technical customers.

## Materials / colors

- [ ] **PLA, PETG, and TPU sample pieces together** — `material-samples.jpg`
  - Label them physically if possible.
- [ ] **Same small object printed in multiple colors** — `color-samples.jpg`
  - Keep lighting consistent.
- [ ] **Flexible TPU part being bent by hand** — `flexible-tpu.jpg`
  - Shows that different materials have different uses.

## Business/trust shots

- [ ] **Clean printer setup / workspace** — `workspace.jpg`
  - Clear clutter first. Show the printer, filament, and work surface.
- [ ] **Finished print packaged in a bag/box with a simple label** — `packaged-print.jpg`
  - Helps people imagine pickup or shipping.
- [ ] **A small group of finished customer-ready prints** — `ready-for-pickup.jpg`
  - Arrange them neatly with good lighting.

## Quick tips

- Use natural light if possible.
- Put the object on a clean desk, cutting mat, wood surface, or plain paper.
- Take both a close-up and a wider context shot.
- Include a ruler, coin, hand, or common object when scale matters.
- Avoid messy backgrounds, harsh flash, and blurry photos.
