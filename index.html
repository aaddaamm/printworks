What 3D printing jobs are a good fit?

Robinson Printworks is a good fit for small custom parts, replacement pieces, mounts, prototypes, gifts, and low-volume projects in Rhode Island. It is not always the right choice for heavy load-bearing, high-heat, food-contact, or safety-critical parts.

Project type Good fit? What to send
Broken knobs, clips, brackets, or adapters Often a good fit Photos, rough measurements, and what the part connects to
Wall mounts, organizers, and household fixes Often a good fit Photos of the space, dimensions, and expected weight
Prototype ideas or small batches Good fit after review Sketches, STL/3MF files, quantity, and deadline
Heat, food, safety, or heavy-load parts Case by case Use conditions, temperature, load, and risk details

Questions

3D printing quote basics.

What file types do you accept?

STL, 3MF, and OBJ files are ideal. I can also review sketches, reference photos, measurements, or links to existing models.

What materials can you print?

PLA, PETG, and TPU are the common options. ABS or ASA may be available by request depending on the part and use case.

How much does a print cost?

The first printability review is free. Printing price depends on model size, material, print time, quantity, and cleanup. Small simple prints are often $10–$25, while larger or custom jobs are quoted after review.

Can you help if I only have an idea?

Yes. Share the idea, rough dimensions, photos, or a sketch and I can help determine whether it is a good fit for 3D printing.

Do you offer pickup or shipping?

Local pickup is available in Rhode Island. Small shipped orders are available by request when pickup is not convenient.
